Cavea: Rules of the stage

Basic Mechanics

Dice Requirements

A lot of d4, d6, d8, d10, d12, and d20 dice.

Level Pool

A Level Pool (LP) is:

  • a measurement of expertise of a skill or
  • a measurement of physical ability.

A Level Pool consists of one or more dice, where each die in the pool is either a d4, d6, d8, d10, d12, or d20 die, and potentially a results modifier.

Examples

1:d20 - A level pool consisting of a single d20.

2:d10 - A level pool consisting of two d10s.

1:d8/1:d10 - A level pool consisting of one d8 and one d10.

1:d4/2:d6/1:d8/3:d10/2:d12/1:d20 - A level pool consisting of one d4, two d6s, one d8, three d10s, two d12s, and one d20.

1:4/2:6/1:8/3:10/2:12/1:20 - The same level pool as the previous example.

1:d6/4:d10/+2 - A level pool consisting of one d6, four d10s, and a results modifier of *+2*.

(Technical: See Level Pool Notation for the formal notation specification).

Rolling a Level Pool

The procedure for rolling a Level Pool is as follow:

  1. Gather and roll all of the dice that compose the Level Pool.
  2. Add up all of the 1s for an unmodified total.
  3. Add and/or subtract any relevant modifiers from the unmodified total for the final result.

Improving a Level Pool

See Skill Level Pool Advancement, Physical Attribute Level Pool Advancement, Advancement Points

Advancement Points

Advancement Points (AP) are the currency used to purchase new skills, to increase a skill's Level Pool, and improve physical attributes.

There are two types of Advancement Points: General and Specific.

General Advancement Points

The General Advancement Points (GAP) can be used to learn new skills and to increase the Level Pools of known skills.

Specific Advancement Points

The Specific Advancement Points (SAP) can only be used to either increase the Level Pool of a specific skill or to increase the Level Pool of one of the physical attributes related to that skill. Specific Advancement Points are gained when the dice in the Level Pool are rolled during a skill attempt. A single SAP is gained for that skill if one or more 2s are rolled.

SAP Gain Example

Rayus the Swift has a 3:d8/2:d10/3:d20 LP in Moving Silently. During his Moving Silently skill attempt, the LP roll resulted in: 7, 8, 2, 6, 9, 2, 14, 11. Even though he stepped on a stick that snapped and gave his position away, he gained 1 SAP that he can use towards improving his Moving Silently skill or his mEn, bEn, or bAg physical attributes.

Skill Level Pool

A skill's Level Pool (LP) is the measurement of expertise of that skill.

Skill LP Example

Rayus the Swift is renown for dodging arrows. His dodging skill level pool is two d4s, three d8s, one d10, and four d12s (abbreviated as: 2:d4/3:d8/1:10/4:d12). Old Gran has arthritis in her hips and cannot move very quickly but is a culinary expert. Her dodge skill LP is two d20s with a results modifier of *-1*, due to the arthritis (abbreviated as: 2:d20/-1) and her food preparation skill level pool is 4:d4/4:d6/2:d8/2:d10/1:d12.

See Level Pool Notation.

Related Physical Attributes

Unlike other games, the gamer's choice of skills for their character and spending of Specific Advancement Points dictates the character's physical attributes. The related physical attributes are:

  • Mental Strength
  • Mental Endurance
  • Mental Agility
  • Body Strength
  • Body Endurance
  • Body Agility

Skill Attempt

A gamer [rolls the skill's Level Pool][roll lp] when attempting to use the skill. The gamer then adds up all of the 1s, and adds and/or subtracts any relevant modifiers. If every rolled die in the Level Pool results in a 1, then 2 Specific Advancement Points are gained for that skill.

Opposing/Opposed Skill Attempt

If the skill attempt is opposing/is opposed by a skill attempt by a separate entity then the total result is compared with the opposing/opposed total result. The highest result wins. The difference between the highest and lowest total results may dictate the type and/or scale of the results. E.g. Defending against a sword attack with a small shield.

Unopposed Skill Attempt

If the skill attempt is unopposed then the GM compares the total result with a predetermined target number. If the total result is higher than or equal to the target number then the skill attempt is successful. If the total result is lower than the target number then the skill attempt failed. The difference between the total result and the target number may dictate the type and/or scale of the success or failure.

Combining Skills

The GM may decide that more the one skill is needed to accomplish a single task. To combine multiple skills, the Level Pools for the each skill are rolled and all of the 1s are added together forming a single result.

Teamwork

Similar to combining skills, the GM may decide that teamwork is needed to accomplish a single task. To attempt a skill as a team, each gamer rolls the Level Pool for their character's skill and all of the 1s are added together forming a single result.

Skill Acquirement

Skills are acquired during the character creation process and while playing the character.

Acquirement During Character Creation

A character's fundamental set of skills are acquired through the early adolescent phase of the character creation process. Fundamental skills are improved and new skills are acquired through the later adolescent phase of the character creation process. Depending on the setting, groups of skills can be acquired during the apprenticeship and advanced education phase of the character creation process.

Acquirement During Character Play

Skills are acquired as the character is played. General Advancement Points are used to *purchase* skills.

Physical Attribute Mechanics

Mental Strength
represents the character's intelligence, learning capacity, and overall mental capability. Abbreviated as mSt.
Mental Endurance
represents the character's will power and mental focus and stamina. Abbreviated as mEn
Mental Agility
represents the character's speed of memory recall, the speed at which learning occurs, and the level of complexity of new topics that can be learned. Abbreviated as mAg
Body Strength
represents the character's brawn, hardiness, durability, and muscle strength. Abbreviated as bSt
Body Endurance
represents the character's fortitude and stamina. Abbreviated as bEn
Body Agility
represents the character's balance, dexterity, and nimbleness. Abbreviated as bAg

Similar to skills, Level Pools are used as a measure of each of the above physical attributes.

Physical Attribute Level Pool Advancement

All physical attributes start out with a 1:d20 LP.

Health and Damage Mechanics

Calculating Health Points

For each die in the mSt, mEn, mAg, bSt, bEn, and bAg Level Pools, look up the Health Point value for the type of die in the following table and add the value to the character's Health Point total.

Level Pool Die to Health Points Table
Die Health Points
d20 1
d12 3
d10 4
d8 6
d6 9
d4 12
Example

mSt: 1:d8/2:d12/1:d20
mEn: 1:d12/4:d20
mAg: 2:d10/2:d12/1:d20
bSt: 2:d6/1:d10/2:d12/1:d20
bEn: 3:d6/2:d12/2:d20
bAg: 1:d4/2:d6/4:d20

mSt: 6 + (2 * 3) + 1 = 13
mEn: 3 + 4 = 7
mAg: (2 * 4) + (2 * 3) + 1 = 15
bSt: (2 * 6) + 4 + (2 * 3) + 1 = 23
bEn: (3 * 9) + (2 * 3) + 2 = 35
bAg: 12 + (2 * 9) + 4 = 34

13 + 7 + 15 + 23 + 35 + 34 = 127 Health Points
